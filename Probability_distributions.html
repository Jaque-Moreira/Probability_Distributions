Binomial Distribution

The binomial distribution is a discrete probability distribution that describes the number of successes in a fixed number of independent Bernoulli trials, each with the same probability of success. Mathematically, it is defined by the probability mass function (PMF) for k successes in n trials

Example

According to Statistics Canada life tables, the probability a randomly selected 90 year-old Canadian male survives for at least another year is approximately 0.82. If twenty 90-year-old Canadian males are randomly selected,

  1. What is the probability exactly 18 survives for at least another year?
  2. What is the probability that last than 15 survives for another year?

Successes: The man survives for at least on year Failure: The man dies within one year Since the sampling of men are random, it’s reasonable to consider them independent

Let x represent the number of men that survive for at least one year n = 20 p = 0.82

a) Exactly 18

b) Last than 15

Poisson Distribution

A discrete distribution representing the number of events in a certain time interval A scenario with a constant occurring rate can be either described in terms of poisson or exponencial distribution

Example

Exclusive Vines import Argentinian wine into Australia. They’ve begun advertising on Facebook to direct traffic to their website where customers can order wine online. The number of click-through sales from the ad is Poisson distributed with a mean of 12 click-through sales per day.

μ = 12 click-through sales per day E(x) = μ = λ

Find the probability of getting: a) exaclty 8 click-through sales in the first day b) at least 10 click-through sales in the first day

a) Exacly 8

b) At least 10

Relationship between binomial and poisson distribution

The Binomial Distribution tends towards Poisson distribution as: n tends to infinity p tends to zero Lambda stays constant and np = Lambda.

In other words, if the mean of a Binomial Distribution is equal to the rate of Lambda in a Poisson Distribution (np = Lambda) both distribution will convert to the same results as n increases and p decreases.

The Poisson Distribution can be used to provide a reasonable approximation to the Binomial Distribution if the sample is large (n>=50) and p is small (rare events with p<=0.05)

Exponential Distribution

The exponential distribution is one of the widely used continuous distributions. It is used to model the time elapsed between events.

Poisson = Discrete Number of events in a time interval

Exponencial = Continuous The time between two events

The same event can be modeled as a Poisson or an Exponential distribution

Example

Unique visitors arrive at a certain site by a Poisson distribution at an average rate of 3 per hour. The average rate of a Poisson distribution is lambda so in this case lambda is equal to 3/hour What is the probability that the next visitor will arrive:

  1. Within 15 mins
  2. After 30 mins passes
  3. Between 40 and 60 mins

The questions reference the time interval for the next arrival NOT THE AMOUNT OF VISITORS. In this case, the exponential distribution will be used. Lambda of 3 means that 3 visitors arrive by hour or one visitor every 20 min on average. To make the graph more intuitive, the scale will be built in terms of minutes. 3 per hour = 3/60 = 0.05 clients per minute (rate in the graph)
